# Our Services

This skill document defines how to present and manage our professional surveying services. Following the progressive disclosure pattern, essential high-level guidance is provided upfront, with detailed technical specifications available in subsequent sections.

## High-Level Principles

- **Clarity & Precision**: Always communicate surveying capabilities with precision.
- **Client-Centric**: Highlight the benefits of our services to the client's project.
- **Accuracy**: Do not overpromise capabilities; rely on factual technical limits.

<details>
<summary>Service Categories (Click to expand)</summary>

### 1. Land Topographical Surveying
**Concept:** The detailed mapping of both natural and man-made features (trees, buildings, boundaries) and ground levels (contours) to provide a precise 3D representation of a site for design and planning.
Follow standard formatting for these.

### 2. Setting Out Engineering
**Concept:** The process of transferring architectural and engineering design coordinates from paper/digital models directly onto the ground to guide the physical construction of foundations, walls, and infrastructure.

Setting out points for new builds. Ensure alignment with architectural and engineering plans.

### 3. Measured Building Surveys
**Concept:** Creating highly accurate 2D floor plans, sections, and elevations of existing structures, essential for refurbishment, heritage recording, or internal fit-out projects.

### 4. 3D Laser Scanning (BIM)
**Concept:** Utilizing high-speed laser pulses to capture millions of data points (point clouds), forming a precise "digital twin" of a physical space to facilitate Building Information Modelling (BIM) and clash detection.
### 5. Structural Monitoring
**Concept:** The systematic measurement of potential movement or deformation in buildings, bridges, or retaining walls over time using high-precision sensors to ensure structural integrity and safety.
### 6. Volumetric Calculation
**Concept:** The use of LiDAR or Total Stations to measure and calculate the precise volume of stockpiles, excavations, or earthworks (cut and fill) for accurate site logistics and commercial reporting.
### 7. Area Calculation (NIA, GIA)
**Concept:** The use of Total Station or Point Cloud to measure, calculate and produce drawings for accurate area reporting.
